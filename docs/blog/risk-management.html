Risk Management Strategies for Traders

February 10, 2026 Risk Management 6 min read

The difference between successful traders and those who fail isn't strategyâ€”it's risk management. Learn the techniques that preserve capital.

Position Sizing

Never risk more than 1-2% of your total portfolio per trade. This ensures you can survive multiple losses without catastrophic damage.

Example: If you have $10,000, risk $100-200 per trade. If your stop-loss is at -10%, you can afford to make 50-100 losing trades before wiping out.

Stop-Losses are Non-Negotiable

Always set a stop-loss before entering a trade. The best traders exit losing positions quickly to preserve capital for winning trades.

Without stop-losses, traders often hold losses hoping for recovery, which turns small losses into catastrophic ones.

The Risk-Reward Ratio

Only take trades where potential profit is at least 2x the risk:

  • If your stop-loss is 10% loss (-$100), target is +$200 (20% profit)
  • If your stop-loss is 5% loss (-$50), target is +$100 (10% profit)

Diversification

Don't put all funds into one token, even if it looks promising. Spread positions across multiple projects to reduce catastrophic risk.

A good portfolio might include:

  • 50% stable holdings (established tokens)
  • 30% mid-risk positions
  • 20% speculative bets

Emotional Control

Fear and greed cause most trading losses. Stick to your plan even when losses mount or profits seem too good to pass up. Use automated orders to remove emotion.
