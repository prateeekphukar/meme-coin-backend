How to Spot the Next Big Meme Coin: Key Metrics and Indicators

Pratik Fulkar February 14, 2026 6 min read
Analysis Trading Metrics Strategy

The meme coin market is notoriously volatile, but with the right metrics and indicators, you can identify promising projects before they explode. In this guide, I'll share the exact framework we use in MemeScout to evaluate and score meme coins.

The MemeScore Framework

At MemeScout, we've developed a multi-factor scoring system that evaluates meme coins across three key dimensions: momentum, liquidity, and stability. Each dimension tells us something different about a coin's potential.

1. Momentum Indicators üöÄ

Momentum measures how quickly a coin is gaining traction. Strong momentum often precedes significant price movements.

Trading Volume (24h)

What It Tells Us

24-hour trading volume is the most important momentum indicator. It shows real buying and selling activity, not just holder count. A coin with $500K+ daily volume has crossed a critical threshold of market interest.

How We Use It

  • $100K+: Minimum threshold for consideration
  • $500K+: Strong momentum, worthy of attention
  • $1M+: Viral momentum, high potential
minVolume24h: 500000 // Filter in MemeScout API

Volume Growth Rate

It's not just about absolute volume‚Äîthe rate of change matters too. A coin going from $10K to $100K in volume over 24 hours shows exponential growth potential.

Price Action

We track price changes since launch to identify coins with sustained upward momentum vs. pump-and-dump schemes. Healthy meme coins show:

  • Consistent 2-5% daily gains over several days
  • Higher lows on pullbacks
  • Volume spikes on green candles

2. Liquidity Indicators üíß

Liquidity determines whether you can actually buy or sell without moving the price significantly. Low liquidity = high slippage and risk.

Total Liquidity Pool

What It Tells Us

The total value locked in liquidity pools. Higher liquidity means you can trade larger amounts without massive price impact.

Healthy Thresholds

  • $50K+: Minimum for small trades
  • $250K+: Good for medium positions
  • $1M+: Institutional-grade liquidity

Liquidity Locked %

What percentage of liquidity is locked vs. can be withdrawn by developers? We look for at least 80% locked liquidity for 6+ months to reduce rug pull risk.

Market Cap to Liquidity Ratio

A healthy ratio is 5:1 to 10:1. If market cap is $5M but liquidity is only $100K (50:1), that's a red flag‚Äîthe price is artificially inflated.

3. Stability & Trust Indicators üõ°Ô∏è

These metrics help us avoid scams and identify legitimate projects with staying power.

Holder Distribution

Red Flags

  • Top 10 holders own >50% of supply
  • Developer wallet holds >10% of supply
  • Fewer than 100 unique holders

Green Flags

  • Top 10 holders own <30% of supply
  • 1000+ unique holders
  • Growing holder count daily

Contract Verification

We only track coins with verified smart contracts on Etherscan/BSCScan. Unverified contracts can hide malicious code.

Days Since Launch

Our New Launches feature focuses on coins launched in the last 7-30 days. This is the sweet spot where:

  • Early investors have taken initial profits
  • Price has stabilized somewhat
  • There's still massive upside potential
  • Scams have usually been exposed

üí° Pro Tip: The 7-Day Window

Coins that maintain high volume for 7+ days have proven market interest. This filters out one-day pump-and-dumps while catching genuine trending coins.

Red Flags to Avoid

Not every coin that pumps is worth your investment. Watch out for:

üö® Major Red Flags

  • Honeypot contracts: You can buy but can't sell
  • Unverified contracts: Could contain malicious code
  • Sudden volume spikes: Often coordinated pumps
  • Anonymous teams: Easy to rug pull
  • Unrealistic promises: "10000x guaranteed!"
  • Low liquidity: Hard to sell when you need to
  • Single LP provider: Can be withdrawn anytime
