Technical Analysis for Crypto Traders

February 11, 2026 Trading 7 min read

Technical analysis helps traders make informed decisions based on price patterns and volume data. Here's a beginner's guide to essential indicators.

Moving Averages (MA)

Moving averages smooth price data to identify trends. The two most common are:

  • SMA (Simple MA) - Average of last N candles
  • EMA (Exponential MA) - Weights recent candles more heavily

When price is above its 20-day EMA, the trend is generally bullish. Crossing below signals potential weakness.

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

RSI measures momentum on a 0-100 scale:

  • RSI > 70 = Overbought (potential reversal down)
  • RSI < 30 = Oversold (potential reversal up)
  • RSI 40-60 = Neutral

Volume Analysis

Volume confirms price movements. A price increase on high volume is more bullish than on low volume. Sudden volume spikes often precede large price moves.

Support and Resistance

Support is a price level where buying interest prevents further decline. Resistance is where selling pressure creates a ceiling.

When price breaks above resistance, it often becomes new support. Bounces from these levels signal strong trader interest.

Combining Indicators

Example Strategy:

  1. Price crosses above 20-day EMA (bullish signal)
  2. RSI > 50 but < 70 (not overbought yet)
  3. Volume increasing (confirmation)
  4. Entry: Market order. Stop-loss: Below support level

Important Caveat

Technical analysis works best on established tokens with high liquidity. Meme coins with low liquidity can be heavily manipulated, making traditional TA unreliable. Always combine TA with fundamental analysis.
